    The First Stop on the Timeline are the 1940s and 1950s

    Time period: 1940

    Edward U. Condon designs a computer that plays the traditional game Nim in which players try to avoid picking up the last matchstick.

    First presented for the Westinghouse display at the World's Fair in 1940. Tens of thousands of people play it, and the computer wins at least 90% of the games.

    Time period: 1950

    Claude Shannon lays out the basic guidelines for programming a chess-playing computer in an article, "Programming a Computer for Playing Chess." that was published in 1950.

    That same year both he and Englishman Alan Turing create chess programs.

    Chess Continues to Evolve

    Alex Bernstein writes the first complete computer chess program on an IBM-704 computer.

    The program was so advanced it was always four half-moves ahead in 1957.

    1960 - 1970

    Who Created The First Computer-Based Game?

    MIT student Steve Russell invents Spacewar!, the first computer-based video game in 1962.

    Over the following decade, the game spreads to computers across the country.

    The Idea That Changed the Gaming World..

    While Ralph Baer was waiting for a colleague at a New York City bus station, he conceives the idea of playing a video game on television.

    On September 1, he writes down his ideas that become the basis of his development of television video games in 1966.

    An Upgrade From Odyssey

    Atari releases the Video Computer System, also known as Atari 2600 in 1977.

    It had the following features: joystick, interchangeable cartridges, games in color, and switches for selecting games and setting difficulty levels, it makes millions of Americans home video game players.

    The First at Home Video Game System

    Ralph Baer patents his interactive television game.

    Four years later Magnavox releases Odyssey in 1972, the first home video game system, based on his designs.

    1980 - 1990

    It's Me, Mario!

    Video game fans go ape over Nintendo’s Donkey Kong in 1981, featuring a character that would become world-famous: Jumpman.

    Jumpman is better known as Mario—the name he took when his creator, Shigeru Miyamoto, makes him the star of a later game by Nintendo.

    Creation of Tetris

    Russian mathematician Alexey Pajitnov creates Tetris in 1984, a simple but addictive puzzle game.

    The game leaks out from behind the Iron Curtain, and four years later, Nintendo bundles it with every new Game Boy.

    Solitaire


    Microsoft bundles a video game version of the classic card game solitaire with Windows 3.0. This was released in 1990!

    Millions of users who would not normally pick up a game console find they enjoy playing computer games.

    Solitaire becomes one of the most popular electronic games ever.

    Video Game Ranking System

    Concern about bloodshed in games such as Mortal Kombat released in 1993 prompts United States Senate hearings on video game violence.

    The controversy riles the industry and prompts the creation of a video game rating system.

    Ironically, that same year the game Doom popularizes "first person shooters”.

    2000's - Present

    The Xbox is born!

    2001

    Microsoft enters the video game market with Xbox in 2001 and hit games like Halo.

    Four years later, Xbox 360 gains millions of fans with its advanced graphics and seamless online play.

    Nintendo DS

    2004

    Nintendo maintains its dominance of the handheld market with the release of the Nintendo DS in 2004.

    Easy-to-use, portable gaming system packed with two processors, two screens, multiplayer capabilities, and a stylus for the touchscreen.

    Games Become Free to Download But Not “Free to Play”

    2014

    “Free-to-play” becomes a dominant business model in 2014 as blockbusters such as CrossFire, Clash of Clans, World of Tanks, and even Kim Kardashian: Hollywood.

    The games achieved sales in the hundreds of millions of dollars through microtransaction payments for in-game items and premium content.

    Exercising While Catching Pokemon!

    2016

    Players hunt for virtual creatures like Pikachu and Horsea in the real world with Niantic’s free-to-play hit Pokémon Go.

    It’s the gaming sensation of the summer in 2016, and commenters debate whether it’s just a fad or represents a bright future for augmented reality.

    The Future is Here!

    2016

    Players truly feel like they are inside the world they are navigating, instead of staring at it through a screen.

    Since the introduction of the VR headsets in 2016, this resulted in the demand for VR games.

    The Oculus Rift S and the bottom is the HTC Vive. These are just some of the devices that are very popular but also quite expensive.

    A Portable Console!!

    2017

    Nintendo’s Switch introduces the first hybrid mobile/home video game console into a game market dominated in 2017 by smartphones and tablets.

    The innovative system allows players to take hit games like Super Mario Odyssey with them wherever they go.
